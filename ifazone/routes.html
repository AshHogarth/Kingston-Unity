Routes to Market

Introduction

Kingston Unity Friendly Society has provided savings and protection products for its members since 1840. We are very proud of our heritage, mutual status and providing for the investment and protection needs of our members. We are a mutual organisation of approximately 100,000 members.

We are based in West Yorkshire but have members throughout Britain.

Setting up an agency agreement with Kingston Unity

Before business can be placed with Kingston Unity, an agency must be set up. There are two types of agency agreement available.

Financial Adviser Agency - This is for Financial Advisers who are CF30 qualified and registered with the FCA. The agreement is taken out in the name of the Financial Advising firm and would cover all the financial advisers working there. We can accommodate firms registered with Networks.

This agreement would cover RDR compliant advised sales but can also cover, if required, introduced business as we acknowledge that in some cases advisers require a facility to introduce a client where a full advice service is not required or deemed to be inappropriate. There must be no advice given via the introduced route.

Professional Introducer Agency - Where a person is not CF30 qualified, but has a client bank for another reason, such as mortgage advising, will writing or general insurance broking, then the Professional Introducer agreement is more appropriate. This allows for introductions to be made on an 'arms-length' basis with no advice given.

Professional Introducer agreements are signed on an individual basis.

We offer a 24 hour turnaround for illustrations and a 48 hour turnaround for new policy application processing. Ongoing trail fees are payable on the anniversary of the plan, usually in the first week of the following month.
